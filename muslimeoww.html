Welcome to Islam

As a revert Muslim, you've taken the most important step in your life. This guide will help you learn the fundamentals of Islam, especially how to perform Salah (prayer) correctly.

Islam is a complete way of life that brings peace and purpose. The five pillars of Islam are the foundation of Muslim life:

  1. Shahadah (Declaration of Faith)
  2. Salah (Prayer)
  3. Zakat (Charity)
  4. Sawm (Fasting in Ramadan)
  5. Hajj (Pilgrimage to Mecca)

Learn How to Perform Salah

The Importance of Salah

Salah is the second pillar of Islam and the most important act of worship after the Shahadah. It is a direct link between the worshipper and Allah.

The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said: "The first matter that the slave will be brought to account for on the Day of Judgment is the prayer. If it is sound, then the rest of his deeds will be sound. And if it is bad, then the rest of his deeds will be bad." (Tabarani)

Steps of Salah

1. Make Wudu (Ablution)

Perform the ritual washing before prayer as taught by the Prophet (PBUH).

2. Face the Qibla

Stand facing the direction of the Kaaba in Mecca.

3. Make Intention (Niyyah)

Silently intend in your heart which prayer you're performing.

4. Takbiratul Ihram

Raise your hands and say "Allahu Akbar" (Allah is the Greatest) to begin the prayer.

5. Recite Surah Al-Fatiha

Recite the opening chapter of the Quran in each rak'ah.

6. Ruku (Bowing)

Bow while saying "Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem" (Glory to my Lord the Great).

7. Sujud (Prostration)

Prostrate while saying "Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" (Glory to my Lord the Most High).

8. Tashahhud

Sit and recite the testimony of faith and send blessings on the Prophet (PBUH).

9. Salam

Turn your head right and left saying "Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah" to end the prayer.

Surah Al-Fatiha

Makkah • 7 verses
بِسْمِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ ﴿١﴾ ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَٰلَمِينَ ﴿٢﴾ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ ﴿٣﴾ مَٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ ﴿٤﴾ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ ﴿٥﴾ ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ ﴿٦﴾ صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ ﴿٧﴾

Translation:

1. In the name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.

2. [All] praise is [due] to Allah, Lord of the worlds.

3. The Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful.

4. Sovereign of the Day of Recompense.

5. It is You we worship and You we ask for help.

6. Guide us to the straight path.

7. The path of those upon whom You have bestowed favor, not of those who have evoked [Your] anger or of those who are astray.

Learn Tajweed (Proper Recitation)

Basic Tajweed Rules

  • Makharij: Correct pronunciation points of Arabic letters
  • Ghunnah: Nasal sound in Noon and Meem with shaddah
  • Qalqalah: Echo sound on certain letters when saakin
  • Idgham: Merging of certain letters
  • Ikhfa: Hiding certain letters with ghunnah
